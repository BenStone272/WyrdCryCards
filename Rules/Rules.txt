What the Game is About
Warcry is a miniatures skirmish game played between two or more players, where each game is referred to as a "battle". 
Players collect and control groups of Citadel Miniatures called "warbands", which they build by picking fighters from specific factions.
Battles are fought according to a "battleplan," which determines the battlefield terrain, deployment, the victory condition required to win, and special twists in play. 
The game is played in a series of battle rounds where players take turns activating their fighters. 
The setting of the core rules takes place in the blood-soaked depths of the Gnarlwood.

**Fighter Profiles and Characteristics**
* [cite_start]Each fighter has a profile detailing their characteristics, which include Move, Toughness, and Wounds[cite: 18].
* [cite_start]The profile also details the weapons the fighter is armed with[cite: 18]. 
* [cite_start]A fighter's card indicates their points value[cite: 20].
* [cite_start]Fighters possess runemarks, including a faction runemark to indicate which faction they belong to[cite: 21].
* [cite_start]A fighter can have up to 3 other runemarks to determine the abilities they can use and other rules that apply to them[cite: 21].
* [cite_start]Specific runemarks mentioned in the rules include the Hero runemark, the Mount runemark, and the Fly runemark[cite: 59, 170, 171].

**Weapon Characteristics**
* [cite_start]Each weapon has 4 characteristics: Range, Attacks, Strength, and Damage[cite: 202].
* [cite_start]Melee attack actions are made with weapons that have a Range characteristic of 3 or less[cite: 213].
* [cite_start]Missile attack actions are made with weapons that have a Range characteristic greater than 3[cite: 214].
* [cite_start]The Range characteristic of some weapons includes both a minimum and a maximum range[cite: 208].
* [cite_start]The Damage characteristic consists of two values separated by a slash[cite: 234].
* [cite_start]The first Damage value represents the number of damage points allocated for each standard hit[cite: 236].
* [cite_start]The second Damage value represents the number of damage points allocated for each critical hit[cite: 237].

**Combat Mechanics (To Hit)**
* [cite_start]When making an attack, you roll a number of dice equal to the weapon's Attacks characteristic[cite: 215].
* [cite_start]The hit rolls are determined by comparing the weapon's Strength characteristic against the target's Toughness characteristic[cite: 218]:
* [cite_start]If Strength is greater than Toughness: 1-2 is a miss, 3-5 is a hit, and 6 is a critical hit[cite: 218].
* [cite_start]If Strength is equal to Toughness: 1-3 is a miss, 4-5 is a hit, and 6 is a critical hit[cite: 218].
* [cite_start]If Strength is lower than Toughness: 1-4 is a miss, 5 is a hit, and 6 is a critical hit[cite: 218].

**Actions and Abilities**
* [cite_start]During an activation, a fighter can make 2 actions chosen from the following list: Move, Attack, Disengage, or Wait[cite: 144, 147].
* [cite_start]In addition to their 2 actions, a fighter can use 1 ability per activation[cite: 150].
* [cite_start]Each ability requires a specific combination of ability dice to be used: either a [double], a [triple], or a [quad][cite: 243].
* [cite_start]If a rule refers to the "value" of an ability, this refers to the score shown on the ability dice used to activate it[cite: 246].
